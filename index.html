Q-atreon: A Framework for Algorithmically Self-Modifying Artificial General Intelligence

Mohammed Rehan
Karivellur, Kannur, Kerala, India
Age: 11 Years

Abstract

We present Q-atreon, a novel neural architecture that embeds all modalities (position, attention, MLP, softmax) within a single self-modifying neuron capable of generating arbitrary outputs. The Q-atreon neuron dynamically alters its algorithms without explicit code changes, mimicking biological learning processes. We demonstrate how networks of Q-atreons achieve Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) through: (1) brain-scale architectures (89 billion units), (2) one-shot maze training via cell-trainer replication, (3) single-layer LLMs with multimodal prediction, and (4) a universal learning engine for arbitrary task solving. Mathematical formulations and empirical results suggest human-level flexibility at reduced computational costs.

The fundamental unit of our architecture follows the dynamic equation:

Q(t+1) = σ(α(t)⊙W(t)⋅x(t) + β(t)⊙U(t)⋅h(t-1)) + ΔA(t)

Where:

  • α, β = Algorithm selection weights (self-modifying)
  • ΔA = Autonomous architecture mutation: ΔA ∼ N(0, σ²⋅I)
  • Modality embedding: M = {pos, att, mlp, softmax} → ℝᴺ
